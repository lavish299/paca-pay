Cyber-bullying: Reasons and Solutions

Social media platforms have proved to be boon worldwide for years for enabling people to learn, share, and grow. But the same platforms have lately become everything but a safe space. There are many out there, who bully others using these platforms. Recently, a group called "Bois Locker Room" consisting of school students amongst the other cyber bullies, have been busted for repeatedly objectifying girls, morphing their images and passing lewd comments. This incident has once again reignited the issue of cyber abuse nationwide.

Even though the culprits of this crime have been identified and legal case has been filed against them, we at PACA, believe it’s important to understand the root cause of such abuses and how we as a community can prevent such incidents from recurring.

Online bulling has been a significant and growing problem, with reports indicating that up to 50 percent of school-aged children experience bullying via technology. (Mishna, Cook, Gadall, Daciuk, & Solomon, 2010.) Some researches state that one tends to bully others in order to gain popularity, feel more powerful, escape their own issues. People who aren’t socially successful may also bully others in order to cope up with their low self-esteem or even fit in with their peers.

Few bullies tend to believe that their actions would not be brought under the spotlight and thus they continue bullying the victims thinking the victims deserve such treatment. In a large study, 40 percent of students who engaged in online bullying reported not feeling anything after bullying online, while only 16 percent of the cyberbullies reported feeling guilty. Moreover, some students reported online bullying made them feel “funny, popular, and powerful”. (Mishna et al., 2010)

As a society, we all must do our part in order to stop such hideous crimes. In our individual capacity, we must stop anyone we know who’s cyber bullying others. We should make them realize the severity of their action and should refuse to pass along any cyberbullying messages. Parents should not only monitor their children’s actions to some extent through parental control applications but should also explain them about the sensitivity of such issues. They should also insist on knowing who their children communicate with online.

Educational institutions play a very crucial role in preventing these incidents too. The institutions must conduct regular workshops and sessions on cyber abuse and sex education. Teachers and counselors should encourage the students to confide in any adult they trust in case they encounter such events. Emphasizing on the medium of transmission, social Media companies should take the required step to block groups that are indulging in cyber harassment. We ALL must stand together in time like these and carry out our own responsibilities against cyber abuse.
