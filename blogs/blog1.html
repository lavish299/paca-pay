Making Technology Fight Cyberabuse

In this era of technology intensive environment, where people rely on technology for their daily chores, the bad coming through it cannot be disregarded.

The more the world progresses toward technology, the more threat it creates to worry about. Cyber-crime has taken ways and turns in menacing the public worldwide. One cannot deny that cyber-offences seem to be more dreadful than the non-cyber ones.

Though we all agree to this, but do we really care enough to take any precautionary measure beforehand? The thing with human beings is that we don’t tend to look into matters or issues until the harm has been done. We focus more on correcting the caused harm rather than working on the root causes and not letting any harm to happen in the first place. We definitely agree that the latter is a better option to work on, isn’t it?

We see cases going on in court or otherwise once a crime has been committed, but what we need to focus here is that even if the offender gets punished somehow, the victim has already suffered and the psychological damage that has been done cannot be recovered from.

Often it happens that the offenders do not even recognise what they have done as an actual crime. Even more, most of the cyber offences are committed more or less because the offender stays in this illusion that their particular action is something to be proud of or something that makes them appear “cool”.

The best way to deal with such issues is to work on the root causes. Technology has become so advanced that if one can use it to commit the bad, the other can utilise it in not letting that bad happen in advance. There are a number of organisations which are working in this direction, developing applications and softwares which identify the unethical actions even before they are committed and warn the to-be offenders beforehand. Popular applications like Instagram use tools to curb abuse by putting up algorithms which identify if a particular comment is abusive. It also uses keyword filters that block out specific words associated with abuse. But most of these features apply after such settings are enabled. (Nicole Pelletiere, Instagram unveils tools to help fight cyberbullying, 2017)

The other way through which technology can be used to prevent such crimes is by e-monitoring, e-learning and other awareness tools. There are a number of campaigns and programmes across the world which focus on these tools. Other applications like MobiCip, NearParent, SafeEyes Mobile, etc. are developed to help parents in monitoring, setting alerts, filtering and customizing various content and features for their children. (Linda Diproperzio, Best Apps and Products to Prevent Cyberbullying)

Most of these monitoring or filtering softwares haven’t been appropriately made use of until now. The aim of cyber cells and communities working in this direction should be to develop such softwares and implement their usage in order to prevent the cyber-crimes.

PACA works in this direction and believes that technology is the most powerful tool with the world which CAN and MUST be used to fight with any bracket of cyber-crime.
