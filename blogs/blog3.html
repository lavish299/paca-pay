Anti-Abuse Laws in India

‘Smartphones inflict crimes’, I don't agree to it. It’s the behavior that constitutes an offence and not the medium.

A cybercrime, who defines it? People around the globe today are using the internet and are in a virtual space with others. India is the capital of most victims falling prey to cyberbullying in the world. Lockdown measures have given everyone a greater time to join the rally of the cyber world. But do all of us tick a checkbox of t&c before joining the internet? Laws and guidelines of being a responsible fellow netizen (internet user) are equally important to that of being a citizen of a country.

Facebook and Instagram have been major platforms for cyber bullies. Cyber bullying will define an offence done by a minor whereas when the same is done by a major it is known as cyber stalking. Cyber laws came into existence since the very first conviction in 2000, lately not the first case of bully.

The punishment imposed for ‘stalking’ does not differentiate between the presences or absence of the “cyber” component under Section 354D of the IPC. Cyber-crimes which are punishable with imprisonment of more than 3 years include publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form, a material containing sexually explicit act and material depicting children in the sexually explicit act under section 67, 67A and 67B of the IT Act respectively.

IPC and the IT Act have some parallel provisions including for the Violation of privacy, sending offensive messages through communication service, breach of confidentiality and privacy, cheating by personation, obscenity etc.

Women facing threats online or facing harassment, even from an unknown person can lodge complain to section 507 of IPC dealing with “criminal intimidation by anonymous communication”.

Any individual intentionally or unknowingly captures, publishes or transmits the image of the private area without permission will face charges under section 66E of IT Act.

Sections like 354A and 354D deal with sexual harassment and stalking.

Trolling or the reason to counter freedom of speech? There are no specific laws against trolling in India and thus in the meanwhile, it looks like that trolling will continue unabated.
